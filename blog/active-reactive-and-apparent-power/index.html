Introduction to basic concepts#

VVV and III are used to indicate phasor representations of sinusoidal voltages and currents. EEE is used to represent generated voltage or electromotive force (emf). Special cases#

PPP or active power or real power is the power that is dissipated in the resistor, in the form of heat energy. QQQ or reactive power is the power that oscillates between the source and inductor or the capacitor. And θ\thetaθ is determined by the nature of the impedance.

Let's look at three cases

Case 1 : θ\thetaθ is zero#

When we assume θ\thetaθ is zero, the load is purely resistive

Code

The Instantaneous power in the phase is equal to the active power.

Case 2 : θ\thetaθ is 90#

When θ\thetaθ is 90, the load is purely inductive

Code

The Instantaneous power in the phase is equal to the reactive power. The power oscillates between the source and the inductive circuit.

Case 3 : θ\thetaθ is -90#

When θ\thetaθ is -90, the load is purely capacitive

Code

In a purely capacitive circuit, power oscillates between the source and electric field associated with the capacitor.
